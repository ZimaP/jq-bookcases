# JQ Construction Standard V1

Status: engineering baseline for the parametric configurator. Values identified
as a **JQ product standard** are deliberate JQ choices, not universal industry
laws. No item in this document should be represented as shop-approved unless the
approval column says so.

This document governs the physical descriptor graph generated by
`bookcase-layout.js`. All dimensions are inches. The renderer, AR, billables,
pricing, review, save, and quote paths consume that graph; they do not create a
second construction interpretation.

## Authority and terminology

- **Published requirement** means a requirement within the stated source's
  scope. It does not prove that a JQ assembly is certified to that standard.
- **Manufacturer application data** applies only to the named hardware or
  product family and is not an industry-wide dimension.
- **Observed supported range** reports published examples or the repository's
  existing supported range.
- **JQ product standard** is the centralized engineering default selected for
  this product. It remains subject to shop approval where noted.
- ANSI/KCMA A161.1 covers factory-assembled kitchen and vanity cabinets and
  explicitly does not prescribe appearance or cabinet design. JQ uses its test
  and toe-space provisions as relevant benchmarks; this document does not claim
  KCMA certification.
- ANSI/AWI 0641 covers architectural wood casework. Its inset reveal provisions
  are specifically written for inset face-frame construction. JQ uses the
  published 1/8-inch value as a conservative fit benchmark for this product,
  not as proof that every JQ detail falls within that construction category.
- NAAWS 5.0 was published June 1, 2026 with an official effective date of
  September 1, 2026. On this document's July 14, 2026 baseline date it is not
  yet the effective NAAWS edition. AWI and NAAWS are separate standards
  families and must not be conflated.

| `carcassFrontPlaneZ` | `0` |
| `finishedFrontPlaneZ` | `0` for `jq_inset_v1`; `-doorThickness` (`-0.75`) for `legacy_overlay_v1` |
| `fixedSeparatorFrontPlaneZ` | `0`; the structural lower-storage separator begins at the carcass front plane for both construction profiles |
| `shelfFrontPlaneZ` | `0.75` for `jq_inset_v1`; the legacy open-front reference is `0.125`; section-context logic must use the centralized setback constants |
| `backInteriorPlaneZ` | `depth - backPanelThickness` |
| `outerBackPlaneZ` | `depth` |
| `baseFrontPlaneZ` | Style-specific: `3` for the recessed structural assembly and `0` for flush plinth/furniture front assembly |
| `toeKickPlatePlaneZ` | `3` for the JQ recessed toe-kick style |

For `jq_inset_v1`, a front spans from its finished face at `Z = 0` inward to
`Z = doorThickness`; hardware attaches at the finished face and projects into
negative Z. For `legacy_overlay_v1`, the front body may span negative Z and its
rear face attaches to the opening/carcass plane. Mounting is always explicit
metadata; bounds signs never select a profile.

Adjustable shelves, lights, and interior fittings use their context-specific
front clearances so closed/glass-front interiors clear the inward front body.
The fixed panel above lower storage is structural: it starts at
`fixedSeparatorFrontPlaneZ`, spans the full section clear width, and uses
`fixedSeparatorThickness` independently of adjustable-shelf front setback, side
clearance, and selected shelf thickness. Open shelving and desk areas use an
explicit context-specific front relationship so they are not accidentally
recessed.

## Construction profiles and migration

### `jq_inset_v1`

- Default for new designs and active presets.
- Saved explicitly in canonical config.
- Uses inset front bounds, inward front depth, shared reveals, and shared
  profile/hardware solvers.
- Default section-door arrangement is `auto`.

### `legacy_overlay_v1`

- Internal restoration profile only; it is not exposed as a customer style.
- Schema-2, schema-3, and accepted schema-4 records restored through the saved-
  design contract without an explicit profile restore as legacy overlay;
  pre-profile AR share tokens use the same explicit compatibility choice.
- A legacy lower-door opening without `sectionDoorLayouts` restores as a pair.
  A legacy tall-door opening restores as one balanced hinge-left or hinge-right
  leaf, matching the prior tall-door behavior.
- Stored price is never trusted independently: regeneration must pass the
  existing fingerprint/integrity contract. Migration may preserve the legacy
  physical result; it must not silently turn an accepted pair into a single.
- New saves persist only the profile ID and section arrangements, not a copy of
  the profile's construction constants.

AR and quote prefill receive the same normalized profile ID and section
metadata. There is no separate AR base/front builder; procedural AR converts
the same descriptor bounds, including hosted lights, into meter geometry.

## Base assemblies

`buildBaseAssembly(context)` dispatches to three distinct pure builders.

### Recessed toe kick

- A central logical `toe_kick_void` records the clear four-by-three-inch
  section in front of the kick plate.
- Physical descriptors may include a recessed kick plate, structural platform
  or sleepers behind it, and left/right finished end returns.
- End returns are excluded from the central clear-void bounds.
- No opaque shadow solid may occupy the void. A renderer-only shadow helper,
  if ever needed, is `nonPhysicalHelper` data and is excluded from layout,
  AR, bounds, collision, BOM, and price.

### Flush plinth

- Touches the floor.
- Supports/attaches to the bottom assembly without a gap or penetration.
- Front face is exactly the finished-front plane.
- Side faces are exactly the outer carcass side planes.
- Has no decorative cap with X or front-Z overhang.

### Furniture base

- Two real front feet meet the floor and occupy only the declared front foot
  depth.
- The front apron joins the feet without overlap or a floating gap.
- The hidden rear support is a 0.75-deep rail, inset 0.75 from both side
  planes, structural, floor-contacting, and separately identified.
- Decorative feet never become full-depth side-view slabs.

Every base descriptor records `style`, `purpose`, `visible`, `structural`,
`frontPlane`, `floorContact`, `recessDepth`, `side`, and
`allowDecorativeOverhang` where applicable. The bottom-panel elevation derives
from the style's base height, and nominal overall height remains exact.

## Front bounds and door arrangements

`getFrontBounds({ opening, mounting, reveal, meetingGap, leafIndex,
leafCount, thickness })` owns front geometry. For a pair, finished width is:

`(opening width - 2 * perimeter reveal - meeting gap) / 2`

Both leaves are equal and symmetric about the opening center. A single uses the
same perimeter reveal without a meeting gap.

Door-section storage is section-aligned:

```js
layoutMetadata: {
  sectionTypes: ["lower_doors", "lower_doors", "drawers"],
  sectionDoorLayouts: [
    { arrangement: "auto" },
    { arrangement: "single_hinge_right" },
    null
  ]
}
```

Reconciliation is deterministic:

- Non-door sections store `null`, never an active arrangement.
- Changing a non-door section to a door section starts at `auto`.
- Split resets both resulting door sections to `auto`, because their finished
  leaf widths and buildability changed.
- Merge resets the resulting door section to `auto`, because its finished leaf
  width and buildability changed.
- Global count reconciliation preserves aligned metadata when the count is
  unchanged; every door section created by a split/merge count transition
  starts at `auto`.
- Protected media, desk, and fireplace zones remain `null` and cannot be
  overwritten by door metadata.
- Invalid manual arrangements are rejected with an actionable reason; they are
  never silently converted to Auto.

Every generated leaf records `mounting`, `frontPlaneZ`, `backPlaneZ`,
`reveal`, `meetingGap`, `leafCount`, `leafIndex`, `hingeSide`, `latchSide`,
`style`, resolved `profileGeometry`, `tier`, and `constructionProfile`. A pair's left leaf is
`hinge_left`/latch right and its right leaf is `hinge_right`/latch left.

## Hardware and front profiles

`resolveHardwarePlacement()` returns drill/mounting centerlines, orientation,
explicit visual geometry, projection, latch side, placement-rule ID, supporting
solid region, and any warning. The complete hardware envelope, not only its
center, must remain on the host front.

The shared profile catalog contains `flat`, `shaker`, `slim_shaker`, and
`glass`. `resolveFrontProfileGeometry()` converts absolute catalog dimensions
to solid frame regions, recessed field/glass bounds, and renderer subgeometry.
The renderer consumes those resolved values and does not calculate a second set
of rail widths or depths.

Profile submeshes remain inside their descriptor's declared render envelope.
Depth offsets must be large enough to prevent coplanar z-fighting but may not
exaggerate the physical relief. Finish-only hardware changes preserve geometry;
type changes select a new catalog entry and rebuild only the relevant shape.

## Validation mapping

Errors reject the candidate and preserve the last valid rendered transaction.
Warnings permit the candidate but require the stated engineering/shop review.
Corrections are used only for deterministic, reported normalization.

### Front and door invariants

| Code | Severity | Invariant |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `FRONT_OUTSIDE_OPENING_XY` | Error | Entire door/drawer front remains within its host opening in X/Y. |
| `FRONT_MOUNTING_INVALID` | Error | Mounting is explicit and supported by the construction profile. |
| `FRONT_PLANE_MISMATCH` | Error | Finished face/rear attachment equals the semantic profile plane. |
| `FRONT_DEPTH_DIRECTION_INVALID` | Error | Inset bodies extend inward; legacy overlay bodies extend as explicitly defined. |
| `FRONT_REVEAL_INCONSISTENT` | Error | Perimeter and stack reveals equal the centralized rules. |
| `PAIR_LEAF_WIDTH_MISMATCH` | Error | Pair leaves have equal finished widths. |
| `PAIR_MEETING_GAP_MISMATCH` | Error | Clear meeting gap equals 0.125 total. |
| `DOOR_LEAF_TOO_WIDE` | Error | Finished leaf exceeds 24. |
| `DOOR_LEAF_TOO_NARROW` | Error | Finished leaf is below 9.5 or its stricter profile minimum. |
| `DOOR_ASPECT_REVIEW` | Warning | Height/aspect/weight exceeds the ordinary product envelope. |
| `INVALID_HINGE_SIDE` | Error | Leaf uses `hinge_left` or `hinge_right` as appropriate. |
| `INVALID_LATCH_SIDE` | Error | Latch side is a valid opposite edge. |

## Pricing and quantity consequences

- Generated door descriptors are the canonical leaf count.
- One valid single leaf produces one door billable and normally one hardware
  billable.
- A pair produces two door billables and normally two mirrored hardware
  billables.
- Push latch produces no visible handle descriptor under the existing product
  logic.
- Drawer and base quantities remain descriptor-derived.
- No inset, profile, or visual-refinement surcharge is introduced without an
  existing business rate.
- Preview, review, save, quote, and AR use the accepted transaction's one BOM
  and estimate.
- Mixed forced/global front styles are reported as explicit style/count groups
  in quote prefill rather than being mislabeled as the global selection.

## Shop approval checklist

- [ ] Confirm 0.75 carcass/front material, 0.25 back, and the actual joinery,
  substrate, anchorage, and finish system.
- [ ] Confirm the selected inset hinge family, mounting plate, cup boring,
  operating gap, opening angle, weight capacity, and hinge-count table.
- [ ] Confirm 4 high x 3 deep recessed toe space, 0.75 kick plate, 0.75 end returns,
  sleepers/levelers, moisture protection, and finished-floor tolerance.
- [ ] Confirm the 4-inch truly flush plinth section and support detail.
- [ ] Confirm the 4.5-inch furniture base, 3 x 3 front feet, 3-inch side inset,
  2 high x 0.75 deep apron, and 0.75-deep rear rail inset 0.75 from both sides.
- [ ] Confirm 0.125 perimeter reveal, 0.125 paired meeting gap, and 0.125 drawer
  gap with physical samples and the chosen hardware.
- [ ] Confirm 9.5 minimum and 24 maximum finished door-leaf width for flat,
  Shaker, Slim Shaker, and glass constructions.
- [ ] Confirm the 84-inch ordinary tall-door height limit, 4.5 height-to-width
  review ratio, weight envelope, center-rail policy, material, and hinge schedule.
- [ ] Confirm the exact drawer-slide family and clear depth for every supported
  10-24-inch cabinet depth and intended load.
- [ ] Confirm 2 x 2 door hardware references, 40 AFF tall-door reference, 0.125
  complete-envelope edge clearance, and the supported wide-drawer policy using
  a physical mock-up.
- [ ] Confirm the 1-inch knob diameter/1-inch projection and visual pull lengths of 3,
  4, 5, 6, 8, 10, and 12 with 0.5 cross-section/1 projection; map each to actual
  center-to-center dimensions, products, and finishes.
- [ ] Confirm 2.25 Shaker, 1.25 Slim Shaker, 2.25 glass frame, 0.125 visible
  recess, 1.5 minimum center field, and 0.75 minimum reduced drawer-frame width.
- [ ] Specify glass type, thickness, edge treatment, retention method, safety
  glazing compliance, and replacement procedure.
- [ ] Confirm each crown profile's front/side dimensions, zero rear overhang,
  continuous returns to the back-interior plane, molding section, and attachment;
  no crown may exceed nominal total height or interfere with lights.
- [ ] Confirm 0.75 closed/glass-front shelf setback, 0.125 intentional open-
  shelf setback, and the 0.75 fixed lower separator spanning full section width
  from the carcass front plane; confirm its joinery separately from adjustable-
  shelf clearances.
- [ ] Confirm 0.125 nominal light-strip thickness with fixture/wiring
  clearances.
- [ ] Confirm the 36-inch unsupported-shelf review threshold against JQ's
  actual materials, shelf thicknesses, loads, and support methods.
